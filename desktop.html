Cute Kitten的人生履历

Cute Kitten

Perhaps you don't know me, but this article can help you get to know me. To be honest, I don't even know which "me" is writing this article right now. I was born in a remote mountainous area, so remote that the place name doesn't even show up on a map. Many of my old memories I can no longer recall. What I can still remember is only my maternal grandmother and some things related to her, and also my paternal grandmother. For as long as I can remember, I lived at my maternal grandmother's home. The daily routine back then was leading the cattle through the mountains to graze. When I was in school, classmates would always call me an unwanted child. My grandmother would always, because of this, stand far away and shout insults back and forth with their parents for an entire day. The thing I did the most was sit on the ground in the courtyard, staring toward the place where the pigs and cattle lived. There was a small path there, because my parents had once appeared from that direction, so I always sat there, waiting. Every planting and farming season, I would follow my grandmother up the mountain to plant potatoes, rice, and other crops. The snacks of that time were wild fruits from the mountains, and other things that were edible or rather, things I believed were edible. Mm, my grandmother seemed to be very good to me. She would always comfort me when my food was snatched away. Once, I ate rat poison that was placed by the entrance of the cellar. The rat poison looked like candy sweet, and also like dried sweet potato slices. I ate almost all of it. The dead rat lying beside it was completely ignored by me. When my grandmother found out I had eaten rat poison, I still remember she was terrified, and asked me if I felt unwell anywhere. I truly didn't feel unwell at all. Thinking about it now, maybe it was because I had eaten so many wild things from the mountains that my body had built up a resistance. It was only after I grew up that I discovered many of the things I used to snack on in the mountains were actually traditional Chinese medicinal herbs. There were many other small incidents during that time, but I can't remember many of them. Later, when I was ten years old, my family came to pick me up, saying they wanted me to come live with them. The night before I left, my grandmother said, "Don't make them unhappy after all, you didn't grow up by their side." I have always remembered this sentence. At that time, I was very confused about why my grandmother would say such a thing. Then I followed a stranger an unfamiliar uncle to the city where my family lived. I was very happy on the way, so excited that I vomited. Mm, I really did vomit. It was my first time riding in a vehicle for such a long, long distance. It was probably motion sickness. As for what happened after that, I can't remember. I don't know why I can't remember either. My therapist said it's because those memories were too painful, which caused me to be unable to recall them. When I entered society, by the so-called nominal age counting, I should have been fifteen. That was when I first stepped into the working world. My first job was at a shady factory. Since I was underage, I used certain methods mm, you can understand it as entering the factory with fake documents. At first, I was on the assembly line, repeating the same electronic component assembly every day. It was extremely monotonous. The factory work was very harsh bathroom breaks were limited to three minutes. No matter what you were doing in the bathroom, you had three minutes. Going over would result in a 100-yuan fine. The daily working hours exceeded twelve. I knew this kind of work had no future. Later, the master who operated the machines that required programming quit, and I volunteered to take over that machine. Sometimes problems would arise broken needles, error codes and I would need to adjust the code to fix them. After switching to this position, my salary was a thousand more than others. Despite that, my take-home pay was still halved. Probably because I was underage, they knew they could bully me. There was nothing I could do about it I could only swallow my pride and endure it. Later, I met two people at the factory a fat guy and a very skinny guy. I can't remember what their names were. The skinny one said he had a computer at home and a shop, and that once we went there, we could start taking orders right away. I was very naive back then. I believed him, and I gave him all my money. The fat guy also gave him his money. As for why I handed it all over the reason was simple: I believed that giving someone all your money was a way to show that you trusted them. After all, handing over everything represented complete trust. Later, the fat guy and I were tricked into going to his place. To be honest, the moment I arrived, I knew I had been scammed. But I was in an unfamiliar place, and judging by how the villagers regarded him, their opinion didn't seem very favorable. As expected, he had no shop and no computer. And so, the fat guy and I, in the dead of night, quietly grabbed our suitcases and ran the entire night, penniless, until we reached the county seat. Once there, we found a random internet cafe and rested. Since we had no money, and the county seat was still within that person's reach, the two of us started trying to borrow money from strangers. Obviously, this was a laughable idea. We ended up sleeping in that internet cafe for seven days. When we were so hungry that we could barely stand while walking, the fat guy and I went to other people's restaurants and ate without paying "dine and dash." We did this for about three meals, one meal per day. Later, the fat guy managed to contact his parents, and they sent him a hundred yuan. The two of us used that hundred yuan to buy two bus tickets and headed to the big city Hunan. The moment we arrived, we found an internet cafe, tossed our luggage there, and went out looking for work. Being underage, it was very hard to find a job. Eventually, I was hired at a Fortune 500 restaurant chain they confiscated my documents. At first, I started from the most basic work. Back then, from the store manager and headquarters management all the way down to the dishwashing auntie, everyone knew me well. I often bought food and shared it with them. I worked there for about a year or a year and a half I can't remember exactly. Starting as a food runner, as I kept working, I took on the cold dishes station and the juice bar as well. One salary, three jobs but I didn't complain at all. As a result, I mastered over a hundred types of juices and cold beverages, learned how to make cold dishes, and the chefs even taught me how to stir-fry various dishes. You could say that what I learned there meant that even if I never worked in this industry again, I could use those skills to cook all sorts of delicious food for myself. During this period, I was transferred from food runner to server. On top of that, I often helped the aunties wash dishes and managed the ordering and inventory system. Later, because of my good performance, frequent transfers between departments, and votes from each department, I was promoted. During my time working here, I went from the most basic level all the way up to the management tier. During this period, I studied management, psychology, and other skills I even took exams. I don't have much formal education, but I know one thing: if it can make me money, I'll learn it. Then one day, I saw a customer holding a tablet. On the screen were things that looked like pillars some tall, some short. I had never seen anything like it and was very curious, so I chatted with the customer for a bit. During our conversation, I discovered this thing was called "stocks." From his description, it was an investment tool. Because I was working, the information I could get was very limited. Later, I went on Baidu to search for what stocks were, and then looked up tutorials on how to open an account. Since I was underage, I had to ask a friend to help me open an account that friend being the same person who had been scammed together with me. He was a few years older than me and had just reached adulthood. Once the account was opened, I put eighty percent of my monthly salary into it and kept only a few hundred yuan for snacks. Because food and accommodation were provided, I barely spent any money. The more I got into it, the more it felt like a new world had opened up. I started researching all kinds of information about stocks, which indirectly led me to start following domestic news, international news, and various policies. And so, I began trading stocks. After working there for a while, I quit and went to work at a high-star hotel. I can't remember its name it was a very long name. I was in a management role. During this time, I met a young woman. She was very smart a server responsible for the hotel's accommodation area. I asked her why she didn't move into management. I could recommend her. Her reply was something I had never heard before. She said, "Work is just a transition. I'm going to take the civil service exam." I asked why. She said, "Working for someone else has no future. Being a civil servant is different it's an iron rice bowl." It was my first time hearing that expression. After that, my plans for the future became more detailed. Besides my main job, I didn't forget about stock trading. The returns from trading counted as another source of extra income. I seemed to have quite a talent for it slowly accumulating. After working there for not very long, I quit again and went to Shenzhen. I'd heard people say that Shenzhen was like picking money off the ground. My first job there after working for about ten-something days, I discovered it was a scam operation. I didn't even dare to ask for my wages I just ran. Later, I met a few young women, and with some savings, I opened a studio for taking orders on poster design, menu design, and similar work. The income was decent at the time. During my free time, I would sell little things cute accessories, keychains, phone cases, and the like. A piece of cloth on the ground was my stall I set up shop right on the sidewalk. Later, I realized I still had time to do something else, so I started researching how to write books.
